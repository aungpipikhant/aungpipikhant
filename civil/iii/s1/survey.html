Chapter - 9 (Field Astronomy)

In spherical triangle APB
$$\displaylines{b=AP=90^\circ - \textsf{Latitude of }A\\a=BP=90^\circ - \textsf{Latitude of }B\\\textsf{Cosine formula}\rightarrow\\\cos p=\cos a\cos b+\sin a\sin b\cos P\\\textsf{Shortest distance}=\textsf{Radius}\times \textsf{Central angle}}$$

Star at Culmination (or) Transit (N+, S-)
$$\displaylines{\delta\le\theta,\textsf{upper}\rightarrow ZS=\theta -\delta\\\delta\gt\theta,\textsf{upper}\rightarrow ZS=\delta -\theta\\\delta\ge\theta,\textsf{lower}\rightarrow ZS=180^\circ - (\theta +\delta)\\\delta\gt 90-\theta\rightarrow \textsf{Circumpolar star}}$$

Cosine Formula for H
$$\displaylines{\cos ZS=\cos ZP\cos PS\\+\sin ZP\sin PS\cos H}$$

Cosine Formula for A
$$\displaylines{\cos PS=\cos ZP\cos ZS\\+\sin ZP\sin ZS\cos A}$$

Notes
$$\displaylines{H\gt 180^\circ\rightarrow A\textsf{ is eastern}\\H\lt 180^\circ \rightarrow A\textsf{ is western}\\\textsf{Azimuth (}A\textsf{) is given eastern, }H=360^\circ -H_1\\\textsf{sunset, sunrise}\rightarrow \textsf{Altitude (}\alpha\textsf{)}=0\\24^h\rightarrow 360^\circ\quad 12^h\rightarrow 180^\circ\quad 1^h\rightarrow 15^\circ\\1^m\rightarrow 15'\quad 1^s\rightarrow 15''}$$
