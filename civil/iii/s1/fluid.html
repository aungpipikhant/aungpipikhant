Chapter - 5

Momentum & Fluid Flow
$$\displaylines{\textsf{momentum}=m\times v=\textsf{mass}\times \textsf{velocity}}$$

Mass of Fluid Flow
$$\displaylines{m=\rho_1 A_1 v_1=\rho_2 A_2 v_2\quad(kgs^{-1})}$$

By Newton's Second Law
$$\displaylines{F=F_1+F_2+F_3=m(v_{out}-v_{in})\\F_1=\textsf{force due to any solid body}\\F_2=\textsf{force due to gravity}\\F_3=\textsf{force due to outside}\\\textsf{the control volume (free jet)}}$$

Pressure, Force
$$\displaylines{p=\frac{F}{A}\quad F=pA\\\rho =\frac{m}{V}\quad m=\rho V=\rho Al\\F=ma}$$

Force Exerted by a Jet Striking a Flat Plate
$$\displaylines{\textsf{Force}=\rho A(v-u)(v-u)\cos\theta\\v=\textsf{velocity of jet}\\u=\textsf{velocity of plate held normal between jet}\\\theta =\textsf{angle between jet and plate}}$$

Force due to Deflection of a Jet by a Curved Vane
$$\displaylines{R=-F_1\\F_2,\;F_3=0\rightarrow R_x=-F_1\\R_x=-m(v_{out}-v_{in})_x\\R_x=m(v_{in}-v_{out})_x\\R_y=m(v_{in}-v_{out})_y}$$

Mass per unit time entering control volume
$$\displaylines{m=\rho Q\quad Q=Av\\Q\textsf{ is flowing rate.}\\F_2=0\rightarrow F_1+F_3=m(v_{out}-v_{in})\\R=-F_1\rightarrow R=F_3-m(v_{out}-v_{in})}$$

Workdone per second
$$\displaylines{\textsf{workdone per second}\\=\textsf{reaction}\times\textsf{velocity of tank}}$$

The Energy Equation and Its Applications
$$\displaylines{\textsf{Bernoulli's Equation}\\\scriptsize{\displaylines{\textsf{Total energy}\\\textsf{per unit weight}}=\displaylines{\textsf{Pressure}\\\textsf{energy per}\\\textsf{unit weight}}+\displaylines{\textsf{Kinetic}\\\textsf{energy per}\\\textsf{unit weight}}+\displaylines{\textsf{Potential}\\\textsf{energy per}\\\textsf{unit weight}} } \\H=\frac{p}{\rho g}+\frac{v^2}{2g}+z\\\\\textsf{Between any two points, 1 and 2 on a streamline}, \\\scriptsize{\displaylines{\textsf{Total energy per}\\\textsf{unit weight at 1}}=\displaylines{\textsf{Total energy per}\\\textsf{unit weight at 2}}} \\\frac{p_1}{\rho_1 g}+\frac{v_1^2}{2g}+z_1=\frac{p_2}{\rho_2 g}+\frac{v_2^2}{2g}+z_2 \\\\\scriptsize{\displaylines{\textsf{Total energy}\\\textsf{per unit}\\\textsf{weight at 1}}=\displaylines{\textsf{Total energy}\\\textsf{per unit}\\\textsf{weight at 2}}+\displaylines{\textsf{Loss per}\\\textsf{unit}\\\textsf{weight}} +\displaylines{\textsf{Work done}\\\textsf{per unit}\\\textsf{weight}}- \displaylines{\textsf{Energy}\\\textsf{supplied}\\\textsf{per unit}\\\textsf{weight}} } \\\frac{p_1}{\rho_1 g}+\frac{v_1^2}{2g}+z_1=\frac{p_2}{\rho_2 g}+\frac{v_2^2}{2g}+z_2+h+w-q }$$

$$\displaylines{A_1v_1=A_2v_2=A_3v_3\\ \frac{\pi d_1^2}{4}u_1=\frac{\pi d_2^2}{4}u_2=\frac{\pi d_3^2}{4}u_3 }$$

Principle of Venturi Meter
$$\displaylines{Q=\left[\frac{A_1}{(m^2-1)^{\frac{1}{2}}}\right]\sqrt{2gh\left(\frac{\rho_{\textsf{man}}} {\rho} -1\right) }\\m=\frac{A_1}{A_2} }$$

Theory of Small Orifice

Velocity of Jet
$$v=\sqrt{2gH}$$

Theoretical Discharge
$$Q=Av=A\sqrt{2gH}$$

Actual Discharge
$$Q_{\textsf{actual}}=C_dQ_{\textsf{theoretical}}=C_dA\sqrt{2gH}$$

Actual Discharge
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Actual velocity}=C_vv=C_v\sqrt{2gH}\\ \textsf{Actual area}=C_cA\\ \begin{align} \textsf{Actual discharge}&=\textsf{Actual area}\times\textsf{Actual velocity}\\ &=C_cA\times C_v\sqrt{2gH}\\ &=C_dA\sqrt{2gH} \end{align}\\ (\because C_d=C_c\times C_v) }$$

Coefficient of Discharge
$$\displaylines{C_d=\frac{\textsf{Actual discharge}}{\textsf{Theoretical discharge}}}$$

Coefficient of Contraction
$$\displaylines{C_c=\frac{\textsf{Area of jet at vena contracta}}{\textsf{Area of orifice}}}$$

Coefficient of Velocity
$$\displaylines{C_v=\frac{\textsf{Actual velocity}}{\textsf{Theoretical velocity}}}$$

Theory of Large Orifice

Area of Strip
$$\displaylines{A=B\delta h}$$

Velocity of Strip
$$\displaylines{v=\sqrt{2gh}}$$

Theoretical Discharge
$$\displaylines{\delta Q=Av=B\sqrt{2g}\;h^{\frac{1}{2}}\delta h\\\textsf{Integration from }h=H_1\textsf{ to }H_2\\Q=B\sqrt{2g}\int_{H_1}^{H_2}h^{\frac{1}{2}}dh\\\textsf{For small orifice},\;Q=A\sqrt{2gh}\\h=\frac{1}{2}(H_1+H_2)\\ \%\textsf{ error}=\frac{Q_{\textsf{large}}-Q_{\textsf{small}}}{Q_{\textsf{small}}}\times 100}$$
