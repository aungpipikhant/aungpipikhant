Chapter - 4

Design of Bending Members

Bending Stress or Unit Fiber Stress
$$\displaylines{ \sigma_b=\frac{Mc}{I}=\frac{M}{Z}\le \bar{\alpha}F_b=F_b'\\\\Z=\frac{bd^2}{6}\\\\F_b\Rightarrow\textsf{Table A}\\\bar{\alpha}\textsf{ and total load}\Rightarrow \textsf{Table 2.17}\\\textsf{Own weight of beam (lb/ft)}=\textsf{Area}\times\textsf{Unit weight} }$$

Depth Effect Factor
$$\displaylines{ d\gt 12\Rightarrow F_d=?\\F_d=0.81\,\left(\frac{d^2+143}{d^2+88}\right)\\\\d\lt 12\textsf{ (rectangular section) }\Rightarrow F_d=1\\\sigma_b=\frac{M}{F_d\,Z}\le \bar{\alpha}F_b }$$

Moment
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{U.D Load}\Rightarrow M=\frac{\omega\, L^2}{8}=\frac{WL}{8}\\\textsf{Point Load}\Rightarrow M=\frac{PL}{4} }$$

Shear Strength
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{The basic shear formula for wood,}\\\tau =\frac{VQ}{It}\le F_v\quad\textsf{(or)}\quad \tau '=\frac{VQ}{It}\le \bar{\alpha}F_v\\Q=A\bar{y}\\\\\textsf{For rectangular member,}\\\tau =\frac{3V}{2\,bd}\le F_v\quad\textsf{(or)}\quad \tau '=\frac{3V}{2\,bd}\le \bar{\alpha}F_v\\\\F_v\Rightarrow\textsf{Table A} }$$

Exact Formulas
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{U.D Load}\Rightarrow V=\frac{\omega}{2}\,(L-2d)\\\textsf{Moving point load}\Rightarrow V=P\,\left[\,\frac{L-d}{L}\,\right]\\\\\textsf{For a concentrated load,}\\V'=\frac{10P}{9}\,.\,\frac{L-x}{L}\,.\,\frac{(x/d)^2}{[\,2+(x/d)^2\,]} }$$

Effect of Notches on Beam Design
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Allowable shear}\\\\\textsf{For square cornered,}\\\tau =\frac{3V}{2\,bd_e}\le\frac{d_e}{d}\,.\,F_v\quad\quad V=\frac{2\,bd_e^2}{3d}\,.\,F_v\\\\\textsf{For taper in transition from \(d\) to \(d_e\) ,}\\\tau =\frac{3V}{2\,bd_e}\le F_v\quad\quad V=\frac{2\,bd_e}{3}\,.\,F_v }$$

Form Factor for Box and I Sections
$$\displaylines{ F_f=0.81\, \left[\, \frac{d^2+88+55\,S}{d^2+88}\,\right]\\\\S=P^2\,(6-8P+3P^2)(1-q)+q\\\\P=\frac{t}{d}\quad\quad q=\frac{b'}{b}\\\\t=\textsf{depth of compression flange}\\d=\textsf{depth of beam}\\b'=\textsf{thickness of web or webs}\\b=\textsf{width of beam} }$$

Deflection (Energy Method)

Consider Shear Deflection
$$\displaylines{ \Delta =\frac{5\,\omega\,L^4}{384\,E'I}+\frac{\omega\,L^2}{8\,GA}\,.\,k\quad\textsf{(For U.D Load)}\\\\ \Delta =\frac{PL^3}{48\,E'I}+\frac{PL}{4\,GA}\,.\,k\quad\textsf{(For Point Load)}\\\\ E'=1.1\,E\quad\quad G=\frac{E'}{16}\\ k=\frac{6}{5}\textsf{ for rectangular}\\ k=\frac{10}{9}\textsf{ round beam} }$$

Neglect Shear Deflection
$$\displaylines{ \Delta =\frac{5\,\omega\,L^4}{384\,EI}\quad\textsf{(For U.D Load)}\\\\ \Delta =\frac{PL^3}{48\,EI}\quad\textsf{(For Point Load)}\\\\ \textsf{Two equal concentrated loads spaced}\\\textsf{equal distance from supports,}\\ \Delta =\frac{PL^3}{48\,EI}\,(3a-4a)^3 }$$

Design to Prevent Lateral Buckling
$$\displaylines{ C_s=\sqrt{\frac{L_e\,d}{b^2}}\quad\quad C_k=0.775\,\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_b}}\\L_e\Rightarrow\textsf{Table 4.4}\\\sigma_b=\frac{M}{Z}\le F_b'\\\\C_s\lt 10\Rightarrow\textsf{Short beam}\\F_b'=\bar{\alpha}\,F_b\\\\10\lt C_s\lt C_k\Rightarrow\textsf{Intermediate beam}\\F_b'=F_b\,\left[\,1-\frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{C_s}{C_k}\right)^4\,\right]\\\\C_k\lt C_s\lt 50\Rightarrow\textsf{Long beam}\\F_b'=0.4\,\frac{E}{C_s^2} }$$

Chapter - 7

Wood Trusses

Checking for Bending Stress
$$\displaylines{ M_x=\frac{W_y\times L}{8}\quad\quad M_y=\frac{W_x\times L}{8}\\\\ S_x=\frac{bd^2}{6}\quad\quad S_y=\frac{db^2}{6}\\\\ \sigma_b\textsf{ (total)}=\sigma_{bx}+\sigma_{by}=\frac{M_x}{S_x}+\frac{M_y}{S_y}\le F_b }$$

Checking for Deflection
$$\displaylines{ W_y=2\,W_{Dy}+W_{Ly}\quad\quad W_x=2\,W_{Dx}+W_{Lx}\\\\ I_x=\frac{bd^3}{12}\quad\quad I_y=\frac{db^3}{12}\\\\ \Delta_x=\frac{5\,W_y\,L^3}{384\,EI_x}\quad\quad \Delta_y=\frac{5\,W_x\,L^3}{384\,EI_y}\\\\\Delta_{\textsf{total}}=\sqrt{\Delta_x^2+\Delta_y^2}\\\Delta_{\textsf{allowable}}=\frac{L}{120}\gt\Delta_{\textsf{total}}\quad\textsf{(OK)} }$$
