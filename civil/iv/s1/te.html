Points and Crossings

Coles Method
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Curve lead (CL)}=GN+G\sqrt{1+N^2}\\\textsf{Radius R}=1.5\,G+2\,GN^2\\\textsf{Switch lead (SL)}=\sqrt{2\,Rd-d^2}\\\textsf{Lead}=\textsf{Curve lead}-\textsf{Switch lead} }$$

IRS Method
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Lead}=(G-d-h\sin\alpha)\cot{\frac{\alpha + \beta}{2}}+h\cos\alpha\\\\ \textsf{Radius}=\frac{G-d-h\sin\alpha}{2\sin{\frac{\alpha +\beta}{2}}\times\sin{\frac{\alpha -\beta}{2}} }\\\\ \textsf{Radius}=\frac{G-d-h\sin\alpha}{\cos\beta -\cos\alpha} }$$
